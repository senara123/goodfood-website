Chicken Soup

Making chicken soup is a comforting and straightforward process. Here's a simple recipe for homemade chicken soup:

prep time

15 min.

cook time

5 min.

serving

6 servings

instructions

Step 1

Place the whole chicken or chicken parts in a large pot. Pour in the chicken broth/stock, ensuring that the chicken is fully submerged. Add a bit of water if needed to cover the chicken.

Step 2

Bring the pot to a boil over high heat. Skim off any foam or impurities that rise to the surface with a spoon.

Step 3

Reduce the heat to medium-low and let the chicken simmer, partially covered, for about 30 minutes to an hour. If using a whole chicken, it might take longer to cook through. The chicken should be fully cooked and tender.

Step 4

Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the pot and set it aside to cool. If you used a whole chicken, you can shred the meat from the bones once it's cool enough to handle. If using chicken parts, you can leave them in the soup or shred them if preferred.

Step 5

While the chicken is cooling, add chopped carrots, celery, diced onion, minced garlic, bay leaf, and dried thyme to the pot. Simmer the vegetables in the broth for about 15-20 minutes until they are tender.

Step 6

Add the shredded chicken back into the pot. Season the soup with salt and pepper to taste. Adjust seasoning as needed.

Step 7

Let the soup simmer for another 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

Step 8

Remove the bay leaf from the soup before serving. Optionally, garnish the soup with fresh parsley or dill for added flavor and freshness.

ingredients

1 whole chicken (about 3-4 pounds), or a mix of chicken parts like thighs, drumsticks, or breasts

8 cups chicken broth or stock (homemade or store-bought)

4 carrots, peeled and chopped

4 celery stalks, chopped

1 large onion, diced

3 cloves garlic, minced

1 bay leaf

1 teaspoon dried thyme

Salt and pepper to taste

Fresh parsley or dill for garnish (optional)
